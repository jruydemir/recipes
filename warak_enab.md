I've been trying to get this recipe right for so long, and I finally got it. Seeing the smile on my husband's face made it all worth it.

Ingredients
===========

* [ ] 1 3/4 cup medium grain rice
* [ ] 1 tbsp olive oil
* [ ] 1/2 large onion, diced
* [ ] 1 lb ground lamb
* [ ] 1/4 tsp cinnamon
* [ ] 1/4 black pepper
* [ ] 2 tsp salt
* [ ] 1 jar grape leaves
* [ ] 4 tbsp olive oil
* [ ] 1 potato, sliced 2mm
* [ ] 1/2 lemon juice
* [ ] 5 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
* [ ] 4 cups broth

Preparation
===========
1. Rince and soak the rice in water for 20 minutes.
2. In a hot pan over high heat saute onion. Once translucent, and lamb and brown. When mostly cooked add cinnamon, pepper, & salt. Saute for another minute and remove from heat.
3. Strain rice mix in a bowl with the meat mixture. Set aside.
4. Soak the grape leave in some cold water, try to sepparate the leaves relatively well. Strain then add some boiling water and soak for 20 minutes. Then strain again.
5. Using a ziplock back split open, place the a grape leaf, vein side up, on the plastic. Place a heaping tsp of filling on the upper middle part of the leave (get the juicy filling from the bottom of the bowl). Form into a 2 1/2" line of filling. Fold sides over. Then pull the plasic up and over from the top. Using a bench scraper to hold the filling down, pull the top of the plastic to form a roll. Shape should roughtly be that of a fat cigarillo. [Technique](https://youtube.com/shorts/Ab7ZXzclyzo?si=rEjECNjE-hpxsv-F)
6. Repeat until filling is fully used up.
7. Drizzle some olive oil at the bottom of a pot. Place slices of potato in a single layer on the bottom. Then layer the stuffed leaves in single layers into the pot. Every two layers sprinkle some of the sliced garlic and a little olive oil.
8. When all the grape leaves are in the pot, sprinkle remaining garlic, olive oil, lemon juice, and broth (only enough to over the top). Place a plate upside down on top of the stuffed grape leaves.
9. Simmer, covered for 1 hour, or pressure cooked for half an hour. Taste test and cook longer if needed. Let rest at least 15 minutes.
10. Serve by placing a platter upsidedown over the pot, then flipping over and pulling the pot off.

Notes
=====

* Filling can be made a day ahead.
* Any medium grain rice will do, but egyptian and baldo rice are specifically good here.
* May want to reduce the rice to 1 1/2 cup.
